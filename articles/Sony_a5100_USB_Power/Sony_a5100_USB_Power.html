Sony a5100 USB Power - Part 1

I have recently purchased my first DSLR camera. It's a sony a5100. It's a great camera for video, but lacking some bells and whistles that can be found on many top shelf cameras. A particular feature that I wanted was a way to power the camera externally for time-lapse shoots or long car-mounted driving videos.

The internal battery only lasts on hour in most cases and although the camera can charge the battery via USB, it will not run the camera on USB power.

My solution is to combine an affordable USB battery brick, a buck-step DC-DC converter, and a wired dummy battery....

The USB battery brick puts out 5V while the camera needs 7.4V - 8.1V. This is why the DC-DC converter was needed.

Total Cost:

$10 LINK ... four converters (backups since it was shipping from china)
$20 LINK ... Wall adapter for camera (I only used the dummy battery)
$30 LINK ... USB brick (20x the capacity of the stock battery!)

= $60

The final product only required wired strippers, a drill bit, a mint tin, a multimeter, and a soldering iron to complete. It took me about 45 minutes. I can now power my camera for hours!
