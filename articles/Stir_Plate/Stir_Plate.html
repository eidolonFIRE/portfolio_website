Stir Plate

This project's function is to stir liquids without any contact with the solution via a magnetic stir bar. Stir plates are most commonly used in chemistry lab work but this one is going to be used for a much more noble cause... yeast culturing.

It works much like any of the DIY stir plates out there. It has a magnet glued to a computer fan underneath the wood top. There's a 25 ohm rheostat connected in series with the fan to control the power.

The power source is a 12v, 500mA wall-wort and the fan pulls 340mA when at full power. At full speed, there's about 4 watts of power going into the stirring of the liquid.

Interesting note: I actually coated the top with fiberglass resin to make it waterproof... that's why the top of the box appears glazed.

Materials
wood - wood Stained pine from Lowes. $7
rheostat - rheostat Radioshack. $5
fan and power plug - fan and power jack from a old computer.
chrome controls - classy knob, light, and switch are from an old stereo made in the 60s.
magnet - magnet backside of a name tag.
wall-wort - recovered from a Nicd AA battery charger that tragically exploded...
