Pipe Carving

I've always hated the smell of cigarette smoke. Then, one day a friend showed me the wonderful aroma of pipe tobacco. I do enjoy a bowl of rich cherry tobacco, but I enjoy carving tobacco pipes even more. I think I've spent more time making them than I have using them.

Pipe 1:

And so it begins...

It is the first pipe I ever carved and it smokes better than anything I've made to date. I chose a churchwarden shape with cedar for the stem and briar for the bowl. The ring is made of aluminum.

Pipe 2:

I made this one as an engagement gift to a friend.

The stem is cedar heartwood, the bowl is briar, and the ring is bronze. The mouth piece was recovered off of a broken pipe and is delrin.

Although this is the best looking pipe I've made, the draw was not the best due to the draught hole being slightly off center.

Pipe 3:

I got a bit creative with the stem on this one but it didn't come out quite as expected...

Pipe 4:

I helped a good friend make one with my remaining briar block. I think it turned out pretty well.
