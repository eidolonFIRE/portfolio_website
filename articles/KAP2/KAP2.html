KAP

(Kite Aerial Photography)

KAP is simply strapping a camera to a kite and taking aerial photos. The art has been around for over a hundred years.

In older times clever mechanical timers were used to trigger the camera shutter. I opted for more modern approach with electronics.

Santa Cruz, CA.


My 9' delta kite and homemade winding spool.





Radio Box:

The designs began with a cardboard mockup and progressed into a custom wooden box. From there, I added the stripped down electronics and large battery. In the second revision of the controller I added a raspberry pi zero, lots of switches, and a small OLED screen for displaying live information such as battery voltages, elapsed flight time, wifi status, etc.

A toggle switch is used to change the screen's feed from the video receiver to the raspberry pi's gui.




Gimbal:

The gimbal plugs into the camera with both the Sony proprietry USB and also micro HMDI. The video is downsampled using this device and transmitted with this transmitter.

The gimbal is suspended from the kite string using a picovet suspention. It's a purely mechanical solution to auto leveling the gimbal.

Micro servos were used for both control axis. The Z axis servo is modified for continuous rotation.




Stratospool:

This particular spool is called a "stratospool". It's a design that's been around since the 70's and has been adapted by many kite enthusiests over the years. I gleaned a lot of info from the KAP forum before designing and building my own.
